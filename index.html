Your Hometown

Let's Talk About Where You Live!

1. If you could describe your hometown in three words, what would they be?

Do you think your best friend would choose the same three words?
What three words would your grandparents use?
Model Answer
I would say my hometown is green, friendly, and a little bit boring. My best friend would probably say noisy and fun because she loves parties. My grandma would say peaceful and beautiful because she likes to sit in the garden and watch the birds.

2. What is the most beautiful place in your hometown? Why?

Do you go there often or only on special days?
Would you take a tourist there?
Model Answer
There is a small lake near my house with ducks and willow trees. In autumn the leaves are orange and red and it looks like a painting. I walk there almost every weekend with my dog. I would definitely take a tourist there because it is much nicer than the busy city centre.

3. What is something you would change about your hometown if you were the mayor for one day?

Would you build something new or take something away?
Do you think your neighbours would agree with you?
Model Answer
I would build a big indoor skate park because in winter it rains a lot and there is nowhere to go. My neighbours with small children would love it too because they could go there on rainy Sundays. But maybe the people who live next to the park would not like the noise.

City Life

Big City vs Small Town

1. Would you rather live in a busy city or a quiet village? Why?

What is the best thing about your choice?
What is the worst thing about the other choice?
Model Answer
I would rather live in a small town because I hate crowded buses and noisy streets. The best thing is that I can walk everywhere and I know the names of the people in the shops. The worst thing about a big city is that everything costs more money, even a cup of coffee.

2. What does your city look like at night? Is it pretty or a bit scary?

Do you ever walk around at night or do you stay inside?
What sounds can you hear from your bedroom window at night?
Model Answer
At night my city is actually very pretty because there are old street lamps along the river and they make everything golden. I walk home from my dance class at eight o'clock and I always take the long way because it looks like a movie. From my window I can hear a cat, the river, and sometimes a saxophone player in the street below.

3. If a friend visited your city for the first time, where would you take them on the first day?

Would you show them tourist places or your secret favourite spots?
What would you eat together?
Model Answer
I would take them to my secret favourite ice cream shop first because it has thirty flavours and nobody knows about it yet. Then we would walk through the old market and I would buy them a warm pretzel. In the evening we would sit by the river and watch the sunset.

Your Neighbourhood

The Street Where You Live

1. Do you know your neighbours well? Tell me about one of them.

Do you borrow things from each other?
Have you ever had a problem with a neighbour?
Model Answer
Yes, I know the lady upstairs very well. Her name is Mrs. Kovacs and she bakes the most amazing apple cake. She always gives me a piece when she makes a new one. Last month she borrowed my umbrella and she gave it back with a box of chocolates.

2. What is the noisiest thing in your neighbourhood?

Is it annoying or do you like the sound?
What time of day is it loudest?
Model Answer
There is a tram line at the end of my street and it makes a loud ringing noise every morning at six o'clock. I used to hate it but now I actually like it because it reminds me of my childhood. It is loudest in the morning when people are going to work.

3. Imagine you have to move to a different city tomorrow. What would you miss most about your neighbourhood?

Would you miss a place or a person more?
Would you visit your old neighbourhood often?
Model Answer
I would miss the little park where I meet my friends every Saturday. We sit on the same bench and talk for hours. I think I would miss the place and the people the same amount. I would definitely come back every month to visit, especially in summer when we have picnics.
Speaking Task

Describe Your Perfect Day in Your City

Situation
A tourist has one day in your hometown. You are their guide. Plan the perfect day together.

Student A: You are the guide. Suggest three places to visit and explain why each one is special.

Student B: You are the tourist. Ask questions about each place. Say if you prefer morning or afternoon visits.
2 minutes Swap roles after
Useful Phrases
Guide:
"First, we should visit... because..."
"It is really popular with local people."
"Would you prefer to go in the morning or afternoon?"

Tourist:
"What makes that place special?"
"Is it expensive to get in?"
"Can we take photos there?"
